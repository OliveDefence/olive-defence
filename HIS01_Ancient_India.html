Section 2 — Indus Valley Civilisation (IVC)
🏙️
IVC Overview & Key Facts
2600–1900 BCE · Urban Bronze Age · Also called Harappan Civilisation
▾
Core IVC Facts — All Tested
  • Period: ~3300 BCE–1300 BCE · Mature phase: 2600–1900 BCE
  • Harappa discovered by Dayaram Sahni (1921) · Mohenjo-daro by R.D. Banerji (1922)
  • Largest IVC site: Rakhigarhi (Haryana) — largest in world
  • Script: undeciphered pictographic — written right to left
  • Crops: wheat, barley, cotton — first cotton growers in the world
  • Traded with Mesopotamia — seals found there as evidence
  • No temples, no clear monarchy, no evidence of iron
  • Bronze Age civilisation — used copper and bronze only

⚑ IVC Exam Traps
  • Great Bath = Mohenjo-daro · Dockyard = Lothal
  • Dancing Girl (bronze) = Mohenjo-daro · Fire altars = Kalibangan
  • IVC people had NO knowledge of iron (Bronze Age only)
  • Largest site = Rakhigarhi (not Mohenjo-daro)
  • Only city without a citadel = Chanhudaro
  • Sutkagendor = Westernmost · Manda (J&K) = Northernmost
Section 3 — Vedic Age
📿
Early Vedic Age (1500–1000 BCE)
Rigvedic period — Aryans, society, the four Vedas
▾
Early Vedic Key Facts
  • Aryans settled in Saptasindhu (seven rivers region) — present Punjab & Haryana
  • Rigveda = oldest of the four Vedas; oldest Indo-European literary document
  • Four Vedas: Rigveda (hymns) · Samaveda (melodies) · Yajurveda (rituals) · Atharvaveda (magic/medicine)
  • Tribal society led by Rajan · assisted by Purohita (priest) & Senani (army chief)
  • Two assemblies: Sabha (smaller, select) and Samiti (larger, tribal)
  • Economy: primarily pastoral (cattle-based) · cow was a unit of value
  • Varna = occupational (not hereditary yet)
  • Women respected — Gargi, Lopamudra, Vishpala were scholars/warriors
🕉️
Later Vedic Age (1000–600 BCE) & Vedic Literature
Social changes + complete hierarchy of Vedic texts
▾
Key Changes — Later Vedic Period
  • Aryans moved to Gangetic plains (Aryavarta) — used iron tools to clear forests
  • Agriculture became dominant over pastoralism
  • Varna system became hereditary and rigid — Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra
  • Women's position declined — excluded from assemblies; child marriage appeared
  • New large kingdoms (Janapadas) replaced tribal units
  • Elaborate Yajnas (Ashvamedha, Rajasuya) led to Upanishadic reaction
Vedic Literature — Complete Hierarchy
  • 4 Vedas (Shruti) — Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda
  • Brahmanas — Prose texts explaining Vedic rituals (e.g., Shatapatha Brahmana)
  • Aranyakas — "Forest texts" — transitional ritual → philosophy
  • Upanishads (108) — Philosophy; Vedanta (end of Vedas); concepts: Brahman, Atman, Karma, Moksha · Key: Brihadaranyaka, Chandogya, Kena, Katha
  • Vedangas (6) — Siksha, Kalpa, Vyakarana, Nirukta, Chandas, Jyotisha
  • Smritis — Law books: Manusmriti (most important)
  • Puranas (18) — Mythological; oldest = Matsya Purana
  • 2 Epics: Mahabharata (Vyasa, 1 lakh shlokas — world's longest) · Ramayana (Valmiki, 24,000 shlokas)
Section 4 — Jainism & Buddhism
☸️
Buddhism — Life of Buddha & Key Teachings
Most tested topic in Ancient India for CDS/NDA/AFCAT
▾
Life of Gautama Buddha — Exam Essentials
  • Birth: 563 BCE · Lumbini (Nepal) · Clan: Shakya · Name: Siddhartha Gautama
  • Father: Suddhodhana · Mother: Mahamaya · Foster mother: Prajapati Gautami
  • Wife: Yashodhara · Son: Rahula
  • Four Sights (renunciation trigger): old man, sick man, dead man, ascetic
  • Great Renunciation: age 29 · left home
  • Enlightenment (Nirvana/Bodhi): Bodh Gaya · under Peepal tree · age 35
  • First Sermon (Dhammachakkapavattana): Sarnath (Deer Park) near Varanasi
  • Death (Mahaparinirvana): 483 BCE · Kushinagar (UP) · age 80
Four Buddhist Councils
  • 1st: 483 BCE · Rajagriha · Presided: Mahakassapa · Patron: Ajatashatru
  • 2nd: 383 BCE · Vaishali · Presided: Sabakami · Patron: Kalashoka
  • 3rd: 250 BCE · Pataliputra · Presided: Moggaliputta Tissa · Patron: Ashoka · Added Abhidhamma Pitaka
  • 4th: 1st century CE · Kashmir · Presided: Vasumitra · Patron: Kanishka · Split into Hinayana & Mahayana
Core Buddhist Teachings
  • Four Noble Truths: Dukkha (suffering) · Tanha (craving as cause) · Nirodha (end of suffering) · Marga (Eightfold Path)
  • Eightfold Path: Right View, Intention, Speech, Action, Livelihood, Effort, Mindfulness, Concentration
  • Three Jewels: Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha
  • Tripitaka: Sutta Pitaka + Vinaya Pitaka + Abhidhamma Pitaka
  • Hinayana = individual salvation · Mahayana = universal salvation · Vajrayana = Tantric
  • Texts: Hinayana in Pali; Mahayana in Sanskrit
⚑ Buddhist Traps
  • Enlightenment = Bodh Gaya · First sermon = Sarnath (NOT the same place)
  • 3rd Council patron = Ashoka · 4th Council patron = Kanishka
  • 2nd Council patron = Kalashoka (NOT Ashoka)
  • Hinayana/Mahayana split = 4th Council (Kashmir)
🌸
Jainism — Mahavira & Key Concepts
24 Tirthankaras, Five Vows, Syadvada — all exam essentials
▾
Life of Mahavira — Key Facts
  • Birth: 599 BCE · Kundagrama (near Vaishali, Bihar) · Name: Vardhamana
  • Clan: Lichhavi (Kshatriya) · Father: Siddhartha · Mother: Trishala
  • Wife: Yashoda · Daughter: Priyadarshana
  • Enlightenment (Kaivalya): after 12 years penance · Jrimbhikagrama · under Sal tree · age 42
  • Titles: Mahavira (Great Hero) and Jina (Conqueror)
  • Death (Nirvana): 527 BCE · Pavapuri (Bihar) · age 72
  • 24th & last Tirthankara · 1st Tirthankara = Rishabhadeva (Adinath) · 23rd = Parshvanatha
Core Jain Teachings
  • Five Vows (Pancha Mahavratas): Ahimsa, Satya, Asteya, Brahmacharya (added by Mahavira), Aparigraha · Parshvanatha had only 4 (no Brahmacharya)
  • Triratna: Right Faith, Right Knowledge, Right Conduct
  • Syadvada / Anekantavada: Theory of relative truth — "perhaps it is so"
  • Two sects: Digambara (sky-clad, naked) and Svetambara (white-clad)
  • Split at Pataliputra Council (~300 BCE) — Digambaras followed Bhadrabahu
  • Oldest Jain text: Acaranga Sutra (describes Mahavira's life)
⚑ Jainism Traps
  • Both Mahavira and Buddha were Kshatriyas, not Brahmins
  • Parshvanatha had 4 vows — Mahavira added Brahmacharya as 5th
  • Jainism is more rigid on Ahimsa than Buddhism
  • Both reject authority of the Vedas
Section 5 — Mahajanapadas & Pre-Mauryan Kingdoms
🗺️
16 Mahajanapadas & Rise of Magadha
~600 BCE · Political landscape before the Mauryas
▾
Key Mahajanapadas
  • Magadha (Rajagriha/Pataliputra) — most powerful; dominated all others
  • Vajji (Vaishali) — world's first known republic; Lichhavi clan prominent
  • Koshala (Shravasti) — king Prasenjit was Buddha's patron
  • Avanti (Ujjain) · Gandhara (Taxila) · Kasi (Varanasi)
  • Two state types: Monarchies and Republics (Ganas)
Why Magadha Dominated
  • Location at junction of Son & Ganga rivers
  • Rich iron ore deposits → iron weapons advantage
  • Fertile alluvial plains; forest-elephants; trade route control
Pre-Mauryan Dynasties of Magadha
  • Haryanka dynasty: Founded by Bimbisara · contemporary of Buddha · capital Rajagriha · son Ajatashatru killed him (patricide) and expanded Magadha
  • Shishunaga dynasty: Destroyed the power of Avanti
  • Nanda dynasty (344–322 BCE): Founded by Mahapadma Nanda · "Ekarat" · first non-Kshatriya dynasty · massive army · capital moved to Pataliputra · last ruler Dhana Nanda defeated by Chandragupta + Chanakya
Section 6 — Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE)
👑
Chandragupta Maurya, Chanakya & Ashoka
First pan-Indian empire · Arthashastra · Kalinga War · Edicts
▾
Chandragupta Maurya — Key Facts
  • Founded Maurya Empire in 322 BCE after overthrowing Dhana Nanda
  • Guided by Kautilya (Chanakya/Vishnugupta) — wrote Arthashastra
  • Defeated Seleucus Nicator in 305 BCE · Seleucus sent ambassador Megasthenes · Megasthenes wrote Indica
  • Converted to Jainism · migrated to Shravanabelagola · died by Sallekana (fasting)
  • Son: Bindusara → Grandson: Ashoka
Ashoka the Great (268–232 BCE) — Exam-Critical
  • Kalinga War: ~261 BCE · 100,000 killed · transformed Ashoka completely
  • Embraced Buddhism after Kalinga · propagated Dhamma (universal moral code)
  • Edicts inscribed in Brahmi script (most) · Kharosthi (NW) · Aramaic/Greek (Afghanistan)
  • Brahmi deciphered by James Prinsep in 1837
  • Sent missionaries: son Mahendra and daughter Sanghamitta to Sri Lanka
  • 14 Major Rock Edicts, 7 Pillar Edicts, Minor Rock Edicts
  • Lion Capital at Sarnath = India's National Emblem
  • Ashoka Chakra (24-spoked wheel) = on Indian flag
  • "Satyameva Jayate" = from Mundaka Upanishad
⚑ Maurya/Ashoka Traps
  • Ashoka's Dhamma ≠ Buddhism — broader universal moral code
  • James Prinsep deciphered Brahmi (not Harappan script — still undeciphered)
  • "Satyameva Jayate" = Mundaka Upanishad (not Ashoka's edicts)
  • Chandragupta converted to Jainism (NOT Buddhism)
Section 7 — Post-Mauryan Dynasties & Sangam Age
🏺
Shungas, Indo-Greeks, Shakas, Kushanas & Sangam
200 BCE – 300 CE · Foreign dynasties, Kanishka, Tamil kingdoms
▾
Key Post-Mauryan Dynasties
  • Shunga (185–73 BCE): Pushyamitra Shunga killed last Maurya Brihadratha · Ashvamedha revived · persecuted Buddhists
  • Indo-Greeks: First to issue gold coins in India · bilingual coins · Menander (Milinda) most famous; converted to Buddhism · Milindapanha = dialogue with monk Nagasena
  • Shakas (Scythians): Introduced Satraps system · Rudradaman I greatest Shaka king · Junagarh inscription = first long inscription in chaste Sanskrit
  • Kushanas: Greatest ruler: Kanishka I · Saka Era begins 78 CE · Capital: Purushapura (Peshawar) · Patron of 4th Buddhist Council · Mahayana Buddhism
Kanishka's Court — Key Scholars
  • Nagarjuna — Buddhist philosopher (Madhyamika school)
  • Asvaghosha — wrote Buddhacharita (first Sanskrit epic)
  • Charaka — physician; wrote Charakasamhita (medicine)
  • Vasumitra — presided 4th Buddhist Council
  • Gandhara School of Art — Greco-Roman influence; first human images of Buddha (grey schist)
  • Mathura School — purely indigenous; red sandstone; smiling Buddha
Sangam Age — South India (300 BCE – 300 CE)
  • Three Tamil kingdoms: Cheras (Kerala) · Cholas (Kaveri delta) · Pandyas (Madurai)
  • Tolkappiyam = oldest Tamil grammar · Tirukkural by Thiruvalluvar = "Tamil Veda"
  • Sangam poems: Akam (love) and Puram (heroism/war) · five landscape settings called Tinai
  • Active trade with Rome — Roman gold coins found across South India
Section 8 — Gupta Empire (320–550 CE) — Golden Age
✨
Gupta Rulers & India's Golden Age
320–550 CE · Chandragupta I to Skandagupta · art, science, Nalanda
▾
  • 320 CE
    Chandragupta I — Founder, Gupta Era
    Gupta Samvat = 320 CE · Married Lichchhavi princess Kumaradevi · Title: Maharajadhiraja
  • 335–375
    Samudragupta — "Napoleon of India"
    Allahabad Pillar inscription by court poet Harishena · Dharma Vijaya policy · Also poet (Kaviraja) · Performed Ashvamedha · Epithet by historian V.A. Smith
  • 380–415
    Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) — Peak of Gupta Power
    Defeated Shakas of Western India · issued first silver coins · Chinese pilgrim Fa-hien (Faxian) visited (399–414 CE) · Navaratnas included: Kalidasa, Aryabhatta, Varahamihira
  • 415–455
    Kumaragupta I — Founded Nalanda
    Founder of Nalanda University · issued Peacock coins
  • 455–467
    Skandagupta — Last Great Gupta Ruler
    Repelled Huna invasions · Gupta Empire ended ~550 CE after Huna pressure
Gupta Era Achievements — Golden Age
  • Aryabhatta — π = 3.1416 · Earth revolves around Sun · wrote Aryabhatiya
  • Varahamihira — wrote Brihat Samhita and Panchasiddhantika (astronomy)
  • Kalidasa — greatest Sanskrit poet · Works: Abhijnanasakuntalam, Meghadutam, Raghuvamsa, Kumarasambhava
  • Nalanda University — founded by Kumaragupta I · greatest ancient Asian university
  • Fa-hien — noted India's prosperity, hospitals, charitable institutions
  • Iron Pillar at Mehrauli (Delhi) — Chandragupta II · rust-free due to high phosphorus
  • Ajanta caves — paintings (Gupta period) · UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Gupta temples: first use of shikhara (tower) · Dashavatara Temple (Deogarh)
⚑ Gupta Exam Traps
  • "Napoleon of India" = Samudragupta (NOT Chandragupta II)
  • Fa-hien visited under Chandragupta II — NOT Samudragupta
  • Nalanda University = Kumaragupta I (NOT Chandragupta II)
  • Hiuen Tsang (Xuanzang) visited under Harsha (7th CE) — AFTER the Guptas
