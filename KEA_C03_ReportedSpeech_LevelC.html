Reported Speech
Direct to Indirect — statements, questions, commands, exclamations. Tense backshift, pronoun changes, and time-word transformations. Essential for Level C grammar mastery.
Level C Statements Questions Commands Tense Backshift Pronouns

What is Reported Speech? When we tell someone what another person said, we use Reported (Indirect) Speech. Instead of quoting the exact words (Direct Speech), we report the meaning of what was said — changing tenses, pronouns, and time words.

Three big changes always happen: (1) Tense shifts back one step, (2) Pronouns change to match the new speaker/listener, and (3) Time & place words change (now→then, here→there, today→that day).

Part 1 — Direct Speech vs Indirect Speech
💬
Direct Speech
Exact words · Inverted commas · Capital letter inside quotes
▾

Direct speech quotes the exact words of the speaker. It uses inverted commas (" "). The first word inside starts with a capital letter.

Structure
Reporting clause + , + "Exact words in inverted commas"
e.g. She said, "I love Kashmir."
Examples — Direct Speech
  • Meera said, "I am reading a book." — exact words in quotes
  • The teacher said to the students, "Open your books." — command
  • Arjun asked, "Where is the Dal Lake?" — question mark inside
📝
Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)
Reported meaning · No inverted commas · Connectors: that / if / to
▾

Indirect speech reports the meaning — no inverted commas. Three things always change: tense, pronouns, and time/place words.

Direct:
Meera said, "I am reading a book."
↓ tense: am → was · pronoun: I → she
Indirect:
Meera said that she was reading a book.
Direct:
He said, "I will go to Srinagar tomorrow."
↓ will → would · tomorrow → the next day · I → he
Indirect:
He said that he would go to Srinagar the next day.
Part 2 — Tense Backshift (The Most Important Rule)
🕐
Tense Backshift — "One Step Back in Time"
When reporting verb is past (said/told/asked), tense shifts one step back
▾

The Golden Rule: When the reporting verb is in the past tense (said, told, asked, replied), every tense in the direct speech moves one step back into the past.

Tense Shift Chart
  • Simple Present → Simple Past  |  "I play" → he played
  • Present Continuous → Past Continuous  |  "I am eating" → she was eating
  • Present Perfect → Past Perfect  |  "I have seen" → he had seen
  • Simple Past → Past Perfect  |  "I visited" → she had visited
  • will → would  |  can → could  |  may → might  |  must → had to
Exception — No Backshift Needed

When the reporting verb is present (says/tells), OR the statement is a universal truth or habit, the tense does NOT change.

Direct: She says, "The sun rises in the east." → She says that the sun rises in the east. ✅

Part 3 — Types of Reported Speech
🟢
Type 1 — Reporting Statements
Reporting verb: said / told · Connector: "that" (optional)
▾
Formula
Subject + said (that) + reported clause (tense shifted)
Subject + told + person + (that) + reported clause
"said" = no object · "told" MUST have an object (told me / told him)
Direct:
Priya said, "I love apples from Shopian."
↓ said → said that · I → she · love → loved
Indirect:
Priya said that she loved apples from Shopian.
Direct:
The teacher told the class, "You are doing well."
↓ told + object (the class) · You → they · are → were
Indirect:
The teacher told the class that they were doing well.
Direct:
Aisha said, "I have visited the Hazratbal shrine."
↓ have visited → had visited · I → she
Indirect:
Aisha said that she had visited the Hazratbal shrine.
❓
Type 2a — Reporting Yes/No Questions
Reporting verb: asked · Connector: if / whether · No question mark
▾
Formula
Subject + asked + (object) + if / whether + reported clause (statement order)
Direct:
He asked, "Are you coming to the shikara ride?"
↓ asked + if · Are you → if I was · question mark removed
Indirect:
He asked if I was coming to the shikara ride.
Direct:
The guard asked Meera, "Do you have a ticket?"
↓ asked + whether · Do you have → whether she had
Indirect:
The guard asked Meera whether she had a ticket.
⚑ Key Rules for Questions
  • Change to statement word order (not question order): "if she was coming" NOT "if was she coming"
  • Remove the question mark (?) — indirect speech ends with a full stop
  • Use if / whether — NOT "that" for questions
🔎
Type 2b — Reporting Wh- Questions
Reporting verb: asked · Connector: the question word itself
▾
Formula
Subject + asked + (object) + question word + reported clause (statement order)
Direct:
She asked, "Where do you live?"
↓ asked + where · do you live → where I lived (statement order)
Indirect:
She asked where I lived.
Direct:
Arjun asked Zara, "Why are you late today?"
↓ asked + why · are you → she was · today → that day
Indirect:
Arjun asked Zara why she was late that day.
📣
Type 3 — Reporting Commands & Requests
Reporting verb: told / ordered / requested · Connector: to (infinitive)
▾
Positive Command
Subject + told / ordered / asked + object + to + V1
Negative Command
Subject + told / warned / asked + object + not to + V1
Direct:
The teacher said to students, "Open your books quietly."
↓ said to → told · "Open" → to open
Indirect:
The teacher told the students to open their books quietly.
Direct:
Mother said to Rohan, "Don't make noise."
↓ negative command → not to + V1
Indirect:
Mother told Rohan not to make noise.
Direct:
She requested the shopkeeper, "Please give me a shawl."
↓ requested + to + V1 · "please" removed · me → her
Indirect:
She requested the shopkeeper to give her a shawl.
🎉
Type 4 — Reporting Exclamations & Wishes
Reporting verb: exclaimed (with joy/sorrow/surprise) · Connector: that
▾
Formula
Subject + exclaimed with joy/sorrow/surprise + that + reported clause
Remove "What a / How / Alas / Hurrah". Add "with joy/sorrow" to show emotion.
Direct:
She said, "What a beautiful valley!"
↓ exclaimed with joy that · "What a …!" → very beautiful
Indirect:
She exclaimed with joy that it was a very beautiful valley.
Direct:
He said, "Alas! I have lost my wallet."
↓ exclaimed with sorrow · have lost → had lost
Indirect:
He exclaimed with sorrow that he had lost his wallet.
🔀
Pronoun Changes in Reported Speech
I/we/you change based on who is speaking and who is listening
▾

Pronouns in the direct speech change based on the relationship between the speaker (reporting verb subject) and the listener (object of told/asked).

General Pronoun Rules
  • I / my / me / myself → he/his/him or she/her — based on who is speaking
  • We / our / us / ourselves → they / their / them
  • You / your / yourself → depends on context (the listener becomes he/she/they)
  • He / She / They / It → usually unchanged
Examples
  • Arjun said to me, "I love my school." → Arjun told me that he loved his school.
  • She said to me, "You are my best friend." → She told me that I was her best friend.
  • They said, "We are going to Sonamarg." → They said that they were going to Sonamarg.

📋 Types — At a Glance
  • Statement: said/told + (that) + reported clause
  • Yes/No Q: asked + if/whether + statement order
  • Wh- Q: asked + question word + statement order
  • Command (+): told/ordered + to + V1
  • Command (−): told/warned + not to + V1
  • Request: requested/asked + to + V1 (remove "please")
  • Exclamation: exclaimed with joy/sorrow + that + clause
🔀 Pronoun Changes
  • I / my / me → he/his/him or she/her
  • We / our / us → they / their / them
  • You / your → depends on context (often he/she)
  • He / She / It / They → usually unchanged
⚑ Critical Rules — Never Forget
  • "said to" + name → always becomes "told" + name
  • "said" has no object · "told" must have a person after it
  • Questions: change to statement word order — remove "?"
  • Commands: use to + V1 (not "that he open")
  • No backshift if reporting verb is present (says/tells)
  • No backshift for universal truths ("The sun rises…")
  • Remove exclamation words (What a! Alas! Hurrah!) → use exclaimed with joy/sorrow
