English Grammar · Reported Speech

Reported Speech —
Rules, Types & Special Cases

Converting direct speech to reported (indirect) speech tests six things simultaneously: the correct joiner, tense back-shift, pronoun change, expression conversion, sentence-type rules, and special cases like blessings, wishes and exclamations. This module covers every pattern tested in CDS, NDA and AFCAT.

Comprehensive CDS · NDA · AFCAT 15 Practice Questions

Reported speech (indirect speech) has six layers of change — every one of them is tested. Work through them in order: (1) Reporting verb — choose and sometimes modify it. (2) Joiner — that / if/whether / wh-word / to-infinitive. (3) Tense back-shift — if the reporting verb is past. (4) Pronoun change — speaker/listener perspective. (5) Expression change — time/place words. (6) Special cases — questions, commands, exclamations, wishes, blessings, and sentences with modals.

Section 1 — Sentence Types & Their Joiners
📣
Type 1 — Assertive (Statement) Sentences
Joiner: "that" (often omitted) | Reporting verb: said / told
▾

Assertive sentences state a fact, opinion, or observation. They are the simplest to convert.

The Structure

said → said that (no object)  |  said to → told (with object)
Direct: He said, "I am ready."
Reported: He said that he was ready.

Direct
The officer said to the cadet, "I am proud of your performance."
Reported
The officer told the cadet that he was proud of his performance.
⚑ Said vs Told
  • "said that" — no person object: He said that he was tired. ✓
  • "told + person + that" — with a person object: He told me that he was tired. ✓
  • ✗ "He said me that…" — wrong: "said" never takes a person object directly
  • ✗ "He told that…" — wrong: "told" always needs a person object before "that"
❓
Type 2 — Interrogative (Question) Sentences
Yes/No → if/whether | Wh-questions → wh-word | Normal word order restored
▾

Questions require two major changes beyond back-shift: (1) the correct joiner, and (2) the verb order reverts to normal (subject before verb — no inversion).

Question TypeJoinerReporting Verb
Yes/No question (no wh-word)if or whetherasked / inquired / wanted to know
Wh-question (what, who, when, where, why, how)the same wh-wordasked / inquired / wanted to know
Direct — Yes/No
She asked, "Did you complete the assignment?"
Reported
She asked if/whether I had completed the assignment.
Direct — Wh-question
He asked, "Where are you going?"
Reported
He asked where I was going.
⚑ Word Order Must Become Normal in Reported Questions
  • Direct: "Where are you going?" (inverted order)
  • Reported: He asked where I was going. (normal order — subject before verb) ✓
  • ✗ "He asked where was I going." — wrong: do not keep inversion in reported speech
  • The question mark also disappears — it becomes a statement structure
📋
Type 3 — Imperative (Command / Request / Advice) Sentences
Joiner: to-infinitive | Reporting verb conveys the tone
▾

Imperatives (commands, requests, advice, orders) are reported using a to-infinitive. The reporting verb must be chosen to match the speaker's tone.

Tone in Direct SpeechReporting Verb
Order / Commandordered, commanded, directed
Request / Politerequested, begged, implored, urged
Adviceadvised, suggested, recommended
Warningwarned, cautioned
Invitationinvited, asked
Prohibition (Don't)forbade, warned…not to, told…not to
Direct — Command
The sergeant said to the recruit, "Stand at attention immediately."
Reported
The sergeant ordered the recruit to stand at attention immediately.
Direct — Prohibition
The officer said to the cadet, "Don't enter the restricted area."
Reported
The officer warned the cadet not to enter the restricted area.
⚑ "Suggest" — Never Followed by To-Infinitive
  • ✗ "He suggested me to study harder." — wrong
  • ✓ "He suggested that I should study harder." — correct
  • ✓ "He suggested studying harder." — correct (gerund form)
  • "Suggest" never takes to-infinitive directly. This is a very commonly tested error.
😲
Type 4 — Exclamatory Sentences
Joiner: "that" | Reporting verb: exclaimed / cried out / shouted
▾

Exclamatory sentences express strong emotion. The exclamation mark is removed, and the sentence is reported using an emotion-expressing reporting verb followed by "that".

Exclamation TypeReporting Verb
General exclamation (What a…! How…!)exclaimed with joy/surprise/sorrow that
Expression of joy (Hurrah! Bravo!)exclaimed with joy / cheered / cried out that
Expression of sorrow (Alas! Oh!)exclaimed with sorrow / lamented that
Expression of surprise / wonderexclaimed with surprise / marvelled that
Direct
"What a brave soldier he is!" said the general.
Reported
The general exclaimed with admiration that he was a very brave soldier.
Direct — Hurrah!
"Hurrah! We have won the match!" shouted the players.
Reported
The players exclaimed with joy that they had won the match.
Converting "What a…" / "How…"
  • "What a beautiful view!" → …exclaimed that it was a very beautiful view. ✓
  • "How clever she is!" → …exclaimed that she was very clever. ✓
  • The emphatic "What a" / "How" is replaced with "very" in reported form
Section 2 — Tense Back-Shift & When to Skip It
⏪
The Complete Back-Shift Table
Every tense and modal, one step back — and when the rule does not apply
▾
Direct Speech Tense→ Reported Speech Tense
Simple Present → "I work"Simple Past → said he worked
Present Continuous → "I am working"Past Continuous → said he was working
Present Perfect → "I have finished"Past Perfect → said he had finished
Present Perfect Cont. → "I have been working"Past Perfect Cont. → said he had been working
Simple Past → "I left"Past Perfect → said he had left
Past Continuous → "I was going"Past Perfect Cont. → said he had been going
Past Perfect → "I had seen"Past Perfect (no change) → said he had seen
will → "I will come"would → said he would come
can → "I can do it"could → said he could do it
may → "It may rain"might → said it might rain
shall → "I shall go"should → said he should go
must → "You must obey"had to → said he had to obey
Modals That Do NOT Change (Already Past-Form)
  • would, could, should, might, ought to — no further back-shift
  • "He said, 'I would try.'" → He said he would try. (no change) ✓
  • "She said, 'You ought to rest.'" → She said I ought to rest. ✓
  • need not → need not / didn't need to (either accepted)
  • used to → used to (no change)
When Back-Shift Does NOT Happen
  • Universal / scientific truths: "The sun rises in the east." → …said the sun rises in the east. ✓
  • Historical facts: "India got independence in 1947." → no tense change
  • Habitual facts still true now: "She wakes up at 5 a.m." → no change if still current
  • Reporting verb in Present/Future tense: "He says that he is fine." → no back-shift needed
  • Past Perfect already: cannot go back further — stays as Past Perfect

⚑ Pronoun Changes — The Three Rules
  • 1st person (I, me, my, we, us, our) → changes according to the subject of the reporting verb
  • 2nd person (you, your) → changes according to the object of the reporting verb
  • 3rd person (he, she, it, they) → no change
  • Memory trick: S-O-N — Subject changes 1st person, Object changes 2nd person, No change for 3rd person
Section 4 — Special Cases
🙏
Wishes & Blessings
May → might / prayed that / wished that — specific reporting verbs
▾

Wishes and blessings use "May…" in direct speech. In reported speech, they are handled with specific reporting verbs and the joiner "that".

TypeDirect SpeechReported Speech
Blessing / Good wish"May you live long!"He prayed / wished that I might live long.
Blessing (formal)"May God bless you!"He prayed that God might bless me.
Wish for self"I wish I were rich!"He wished that he were rich.
Curse / Ill-wish"May you fail!"He cursed / wished ill that I might fail.
More Examples
  • "May you succeed!" → She wished/prayed that he might succeed. ✓
  • "May God bless you with happiness!" → He prayed that God might bless her with happiness. ✓
  • "I wish I could fly!" → He wished that he could fly. ✓
🎭
Optative & Exclamatory — Emotions & Greetings
Hello, Good morning, Congratulations, Alas, Bravo → all have specific reported forms
▾
Direct SpeechReporting Verb / Structure
"Hello!" / "Hi!" / "Good morning!"greeted / wished (name) good morning
"Goodbye!" / "Farewell!"bade (name) goodbye / farewell
"Congratulations!"congratulated (name) on (noun/gerund)
"Thank you!"thanked (name) for (noun/gerund)
"Sorry!" / "I apologise!"apologised to (name) for (noun/gerund)
"Hurrah!" / "Bravo!"exclaimed with joy that…
"Alas!" / "Oh no!"exclaimed with sorrow / lamented that…
"What a…!" / "How…!"exclaimed with admiration/surprise that…
Direct
"Congratulations on passing the NDA examination!" said the colonel to the cadet.
Reported
The colonel congratulated the cadet on passing the NDA examination.
Direct
"Thank you for your bravery," said the general to the soldier.
Reported
The general thanked the soldier for his bravery.
⚡
Special Cases — Let, If Only, It's Time, Would Rather
Fixed structures that need special treatment in reported speech
▾
Direct SpeechReported Form
"Let's go to the range."He proposed / suggested that they should go to the range. ✓
"Let him enter."He ordered / allowed that he should enter. ✓
"If only I had won!"He wished that he had won. ✓
"It's high time you worked."He said it was high time I worked. ✓
"I would rather stay."He said he would rather stay. (no change to "would rather") ✓
"I would sooner die."He said he would sooner die. (no change) ✓
"Let's" — Proposal / Suggestion
  • "Let's plan the operation." → He suggested that they should plan the operation. ✓
  • "Let's" always becomes a suggestion/proposal — never a command in reported speech
⚑ "Must" in Reported Speech — Two Meanings
  • Obligation must → had to: "You must report by 0600." → He said I had to report by 0600. ✓
  • Logical deduction must → must (no change): "He must be the captain." → He said he must be the captain. ✓
  • Read the context to determine which "must" is being used before converting
Reported Speech — Complete Quick Reference
Joiners by Sentence Type
  • Statement: that (often dropped) → told / said that
  • Yes/No Question: if / whether → asked if/whether
  • Wh-Question: same wh-word → asked what/where/when…
  • Command/Request: to-infinitive → ordered/requested to…
  • Prohibition: not to-infinitive → warned/told not to…
  • Exclamation/Wish: that → exclaimed / wished / prayed that
Key Reporting Verbs
  • said/told → statements (said = no object; told = needs object)
  • asked/inquired → questions
  • ordered/commanded → strong commands
  • requested/begged/implored → polite requests
  • advised/warned/cautioned → advice/warning
  • suggested/proposed → always + that…should (never + to-inf.)
  • exclaimed with joy/sorrow/surprise → exclamations
  • wished/prayed → blessings and wishes
  • congratulated on / thanked for / apologised for → greetings
  • greeted / bade farewell → greetings/farewells
Tense Back-Shift (Key Pairs)
  • Simple Present → Simple Past
  • Present Continuous → Past Continuous
  • Present Perfect → Past Perfect
  • Simple Past → Past Perfect
  • will → would | can → could | may → might
  • shall → should | must (obligation) → had to
  • No change: would, could, should, might, ought to, used to, Past Perfect
Expression Changes
  • now → then | today → that day | tonight → that night
  • yesterday → the previous day / the day before
  • tomorrow → the next day / the following day
  • last week → the previous week | next week → the following week
  • ago → before / previously
  • here → there | this → that | these → those
Special Cases at a Glance
  • "May…" blessing: prayed/wished that…might
  • "Congratulations!": congratulated on + gerund
  • "Thank you!": thanked for + gerund/noun
  • "Sorry!": apologised to…for + gerund
  • "Let's…": proposed/suggested that they should…
  • "must" obligation: → had to | "must" deduction: → must (no change)
  • "would rather / sooner": no change
  • Exclamations "What a / How": → "very" in reported form
When Back-Shift Does NOT Apply
  • Universal/scientific truths: the sun rises (stays present) ✓
  • Historical facts: India became independent in 1947 ✓
  • Reporting verb is Present/Future tense (no back-shift needed)
  • Modal already in past form (would/could/should/might/ought to)
  • Past Perfect: already at the furthest back — no further shift
  • Pronouns: 3rd person (he/she/it/they) never changes
