Topic 1 — Operation Sindoor (Most Important)
🎯
Operation Sindoor — Complete Summary
India's biggest military action since 1971 · May 2025
▾
🔴 Operation Sindoor — Key Facts
  • Trigger: Pahalgam terror attack on 22 April 2025 — 26 civilians killed (targeted on religious grounds) in Jammu & Kashmir
  • Operation launched: 7 May 2025 (night of 6–7 May) — India's tri-services military action
  • Targets hit: 9 terror infrastructure sites — including JeM HQ in Bahawalpur & LeT's Muridke training facility, in Pakistan and PoK
  • Duration: 7 May to 10 May 2025 (4 days)
  • Ceasefire: Pakistan DGMO called Indian DGMO — ceasefire effective from 1700 hrs IST on 10 May 2025
  • Terror groups targeted: Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) and Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT)
  • Weapons used by India: BrahMos cruise missiles, SCALP-EG missiles, Harop anti-radiation drones, Crystal Maze & Rampage rockets
  • Air defence star: Indigenous Akashteer system neutralised hundreds of Pakistani drones and missiles
  • Pakistan's Operation: Pakistan called its retaliation Operation Bunyan-un-Marsoos
  • India's new doctrine (PM Modi, 12 May): Any terror attack = assured retaliation · No nuclear blackmail · No distinction between terrorists and harbouring state · "Terror and talks cannot go together"
  • Indus Waters Treaty — India suspended the treaty ("Blood and water cannot flow together")
  • Attari-Wagah border — closed by India
  • Pakistan promoted Army Chief Asim Munir to Field Marshal (20 May 2025)
⚑ Exam Traps — Don't Get These Wrong
  • Pahalgam attack = 22 April 2025 · Operation Sindoor launched = 7 May 2025
  • Targets: 9 sites — terrorist infrastructure only, not Pakistan military/civilian initially
  • India struck 11 Pakistani airbases in the later phase (9–10 May)
  • Ceasefire: Pakistan's DGMO contacted India's DGMO (not the other way around)
  • Akashteer = Indigenous air defence system that intercepted drones · Different from S-400
  • Operation Sindoor is described as India's most significant military action since the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War

Chiefs of the Armed Forces (Current)
CDS, Army, Navy, Air Force — who holds what as of 2026

    Gen Anil Chauhan — Chief of Defence Staff HOT
    India's 2nd CDS · Appointed 28 Sep 2022 · Extension granted by ACC on 24 Sep 2025 — tenure extended till 30 May 2026 · Also serves as Secretary, Dept of Military Affairs · "China Expert" with multiple postings in Eastern Command · Was Director General of Military Operations (DGMO) during Balakot airstrike 2019
  • Army
    Gen Upendra Dwivedi — Chief of Army Staff (COAS)
    Current Chief of Army Staff · Scheduled to retire in July 2026 on reaching age 62 · Led the Army's role in Operation Sindoor
  • Navy
    Admiral Dinesh Kumar Tripathi — Chief of Naval Staff (CNS)
    Current Chief of Naval Staff · Scheduled to retire in May 2026 on reaching age 62
  • IAF
    Air Chief Marshal A.P. Singh — Chief of Air Staff (CAS)
    Took charge on 30 Sep 2024 · Scheduled to serve until 31 Oct 2026 · Led IAF during Operation Sindoor
Key Context — Why This Matters
  • With CDS Chauhan retiring May 2026, Army Chief retiring July 2026 and Navy Chief retiring May 2026 — India faces multiple top-level transitions in one window
  • India's 1st CDS was Gen Bipin Rawat — died in a helicopter crash near Coonoor, Tamil Nadu on 8 December 2021
  • The post of CDS was created in January 2020 on recommendation of Kargil Review Committee

Air Force — Tejas & Weapons
HAL Tejas Mk1A — India's biggest indigenous defence contract
▾
  • Sep 2025
    97 Tejas Mk1A aircraft ordered — India's biggest ever indigenous defence contract HOT
    MoD contracted HAL for 97 Tejas Mk1A (68 single-seater + 29 twin-seater) · Contract worth ~₹1.1 lakh crore combined with earlier 83-aircraft deal · Indigenous content over 64% · Deliveries begin 2027–28 · Replaces MiG-21 fleet · Features: UTTAM AESA Radar, Astra BVR missiles, ASRAAM close-combat missiles
  • Mar 2025
    Astra Mk1 missile successfully tested from Tejas
    Direct hit on target over 100 km away on 12 March 2025 · Astra = India's indigenous BVR (Beyond Visual Range) air-to-air missile developed by DRDO · Being integrated on Tejas, Su-30MKI, Navy's MiG-29
  • Ongoing
    Astra Mk2 integration on Tejas — trials expected mid-2026
    Range: 140–160 km · Being integrated on Tejas Mk1 and Mk1A · Full production by 2027 · Cost: ₹7 crore vs ₹25 crore for European Meteor missile

BrahMos — Key Facts
  • BrahMos = India + Russia joint cruise missile · Speed Mach 3 (upgradable to Mach 7 with BrahMos-II)
  • Range: 290–600 km · Warhead: 300 kg · Deployed on all 3 services (land, sea, air)
  • India exported BrahMos to Philippines in 2025 — first export of BrahMos
  • BrahMos production unit inaugurated in Lucknow
  • BrahMos-NG (Next Generation) = smaller, lighter · For Tejas and Rafale · Under development

DRDO Milestones & Other Inductions 2025
Indigenous weapons, air defence, and defence reforms
▾
  • Apr 2025
    IDD&IS Mk-IIA (Sahastra Shakti) tested — laser-based anti-drone system
    Directed Energy Weapon (DEW) · 30 kW power, 5 km range · Also called Sahastra Shakti · For counter-drone (C-UAS) role · Indian Army already has Mk-I (7 units) inducted 2024

    Ashwini radar deal — ₹2,906 crore with Bharat Electronics Limited
    MoD signed deal for 18 Ashwini low-level tracking radars on 12 March 2025 · AESA-based radar for air defence · BEL (Bharat Electronics Limited) to supply
  • 2025
    Akash-NG (Next Generation) surface-to-air missile inducted
    Range: 80 km · Weight: 350 kg · Ku-band radar seeker · Precision against drones and stealth targets · Developed by DRDO
  • 2025
    India's defence exports reach ~₹21,083 crore ($2.5 billion) in FY 2023–24
    32.5% jump from previous year · Goal: $5 billion exports by 2025 · India = 2nd largest military by active personnel (1.44 million) · 3rd largest defence budget (₹6.21 lakh crore ≈ $74 billion for 2025–26)
  • 2025
    2025 declared "Year of Reforms" by Ministry of Defence
    Focus on Jointness & Integration · Establishing Integrated Theatre Commands (ITC) · MoD also cleared High-Altitude Pseudo-Satellite (AS-HAPS) for IAF in Feb 2026
