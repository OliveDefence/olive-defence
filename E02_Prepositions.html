Time Prepositions — AT / IN / ON
The exact rules + the cases that trick everyone
▾
PrepositionUsed withExamples
ATExact time · Meal times · Night/Dawn/Noon/Midnight · Festivals (some)at 5 pm · at noon · at midnight · at dawn · at breakfast · at Christmas · at Easter · at the weekend (BrE)
INMonths · Years · Seasons · Centuries · Long periods · Parts of day (morning/afternoon/evening)in January · in 2025 · in summer · in the morning · in the 21st century · in the past · in the future
ONDays · Dates · Specific mornings/evenings (with day name)on Monday · on 15 August · on Christmas Day · on the morning of 5th · on time (punctual)
⚑ High-Frequency Exam Traps
  • ✗ in night → ✓ at night (always "at night")
  • ✗ on morning → ✓ in the morning BUT on Monday morning (day + morning = ON)
  • ✗ at the morning → ✓ in the morning
  • ✗ in time (punctual) → ✓ on time · "in time" = before deadline (both correct but different meanings)
  • ✗ at the weekend (AmE) → ✓ at the weekend (BrE) / on the weekend (AmE) — exams use BrE
  • No preposition before: last, next, this, every → ✗ "on last Monday" → ✓ "last Monday"

⚑ Classic Exam Traps — Transport
  • on the bus / train / plane / ship / bicycle → (open/flat transport)
  • in the car / taxi / lift (elevator) → (enclosed, small)
  • ✗ in the bus → ✓ on the bus
  • ✗ on the car → ✓ in the car
  • "in hospital" (BrE, as a patient) vs "at the hospital" (visiting)
  • "at school" = attending as student · "in school" = inside the building

⚑ "Revert back" — Classic CDS/NDA Error

"Revert" already means to go back. "Revert back" is redundant. The correct form is simply "revert to" — this error appears in almost every CDS sentence correction paper.

⚑ Most Tested Pair — Superior/Inferior/Junior/Senior

These four words ALWAYS take "to" — NEVER "than". This mistake is tested in EVERY CDS paper: ✗ "He is senior than me" → ✓ "He is senior to me"

Phrasal Verbs with BREAK, BRING, CALL, CARRY
Most used group — most frequently tested
▾
  • Break down
    1. Stop functioning · 2. Lose emotional control · 3. Analyse into parts
    The car broke down on the highway · She broke down in tears · Break down the problem into steps
  • Break in / into
    Enter by force · Interrupt a conversation
    Thieves broke into the house · He broke in while she was talking
  • Break off
    Stop suddenly · End a relationship/engagement
    The negotiations broke off · She broke off the engagement
  • Break out
    Escape · Begin suddenly (war, fire, disease)
    Prisoners broke out · War broke out in 1939
  • Break through
    Achieve a major advance / overcome a barrier
    Scientists broke through in cancer research
  • Break up
    End a relationship · Dissolve/disperse · School term ends
    They broke up after 5 years · Police broke up the crowd
  • Bring about
    Cause (something) to happen
    The treaty brought about peace
  • Bring forward
    Move to an earlier time · Propose
    The meeting was brought forward to Monday
  • Bring out
    Publish · Make visible · Reveal a quality
    The publisher brought out a new edition · Hardship brings out the best in people
  • Bring round / around
    Persuade · Restore to consciousness
    I brought him round to my view · The doctor brought him round
  • Bring up
    Raise children · Mention a topic · Vomit
    She was brought up in Delhi · He brought up the question again
  • Call for
    Demand/require · Go to collect
    The situation calls for urgent action · I'll call for you at 8
  • Call off
    Cancel
    The match was called off due to rain
  • Call on / upon
    Visit (briefly) · Request/appeal formally
    Called on the minister · Called upon to address the nation
  • Carry on
    Continue · Conduct (business)
    Carry on with your work · Carries on a business
  • Carry out
    Perform/execute (a task, plan, order)
    Carry out the instructions · The operation was carried out successfully
  • Carry through
    Complete successfully despite difficulties
    Her determination carried her through the crisis

Phrasal Verbs with COME, CUT, FALL, GET
Critical group — meaning changes completely with each particle
▾
  • Come across
    Encounter unexpectedly · Seem / make an impression
    I came across an old letter · She comes across as confident
  • Come about
    Happen (especially unexpectedly)
    How did this situation come about?
  • Come off
    Succeed · Detach · (Stain) be removed
    The plan came off well · The button came off
  • Come round / around
    Regain consciousness · Be persuaded · Recur (date)
    She came round after 10 minutes · He finally came round to our view
  • Come up with
    Produce / find (idea, solution)
    She came up with a brilliant idea
  • Cut down on
    Reduce consumption
    Cut down on sugar · Cut down expenses
  • Cut off
    Disconnect · Interrupt · Isolate
    The village was cut off by floods · He was cut off mid-sentence
  • Cut out
    Remove by cutting · Stop doing (habit) · Engine stops
    Cut out the middleman · Cut out junk food · The engine cut out
  • Fall back on
    Use as a last resort
    He had savings to fall back on
  • Fall in with
    Agree with · Join by chance
    He fell in with our plans · Fell in with bad company
  • Fall out
    Quarrel · Fall (from position) · Happen (result)
    The brothers fell out over property · It fell out that she was right
  • Fall through
    Fail / not materialise
    The deal fell through at the last minute
  • Get across
    Communicate successfully · Cross over
    She couldn't get her point across
  • Get along / on with
    Have a good relationship with · Make progress
    They get along well · How are you getting on with the project?
  • Get at
    Imply/hint · Criticise repeatedly · Reach
    What are you getting at? · She was always getting at him
  • Get away with
    Escape consequences
    He got away with cheating
  • Get over
    Recover from · Overcome · Communicate
    Got over the illness · Getting over the problem
  • Get round / around
    Find a way to avoid an obstacle · Persuade
    Got round the regulation · She can always get round him

All high-frequency fixed prepositions and common errors in one place — scan this before your exam.

⚑ NEVER "than" — Always "to"
  • Superior to · Inferior to · Senior to · Junior to
  • Prior to · Preferable to · Anterior to · Posterior to
  • ✗ He is senior than me → ✓ senior to me
  • ✗ This is preferable than that → ✓ preferable to
⚑ Always "of" — Not "for/from"
  • Accused of · Acquitted of · Convicted of
  • Guilty of · Proud of · Fond of · Capable of
  • Suspicious of · Worthy of · Envious of
  • Consist of · Boast of · Remind of
  • ✗ charged of → ✓ charged with
⚑ Always "with" — Not "to/by"
  • Charged with · Comply with · Tamper with
  • Disgusted with (person) · Pleased with
  • Interfere with (obstruct) · Popular with
  • ✗ comply to → ✓ comply with
  • ✗ married with → ✓ married to
⚑ Confusable Pairs — Both Right, Different Meanings
  • Agree with (person) · Agree to (proposal) · Agree on (topic)
  • Tired of (bored) · Tired from (exhausted)
  • Anxious about (worried) · Anxious for (eager)
  • Result in (cause) · Result from (caused by)
  • Deal in (trade) · Deal with (handle)
  • Differ from (different) · Differ with (disagree)
⚑ Always "from" — Not "of/to"
  • Abstain from · Refrain from · Desist from
  • Prohibit from · Deter from · Dissuade from
  • Exempt from · Withdraw from · Differ from
  • ✗ refrain of → ✓ refrain from
  • ✗ abstain of → ✓ abstain from
⚑ "Revert back" and Other Redundancy Errors
  • ✗ Revert back → ✓ revert to ("revert" = go back)
  • ✗ Return back → ✓ return
  • ✗ Repeat again → ✓ repeat
  • ✗ Dispose off → ✓ dispose of
  • ✗ in night → ✓ at night
  • ✗ on last Monday → ✓ last Monday
Key Phrasal Verb Meanings
  • Call off = cancel · Put off = postpone/discourage
  • Bring about = cause · Bring off = succeed against odds
  • Give in = yield · Give up = abandon completely
  • Fall through = fail/not happen · Fall out = quarrel
  • Break out = escape/begin suddenly · Break off = stop/end
  • Go off = explode/ring/spoil · Go back on = break promise
  • Run into = meet by chance/encounter problem
  • Set up = establish · Set aside = reserve/overturn
Transport Prepositions — Quick Fix
  • ON the bus / train / plane / ship / bicycle / horse
  • IN the car / taxi / van / lift (elevator)
  • ✗ in the bus → ✓ on the bus
  • ✗ on the car → ✓ in the car
  • AT night · IN the morning · ON Monday morning
  • No prep before: last, next, this, every
